Smart Thermostat Buying Guide

Do You Need a C-Wire?

Many older homes don't have a C-wire (common wire) running to their thermostat. Most modern smart thermostats need one for continuous power. The good news: Nest includes a Power Connector, Ecobee includes a Power Extender Kit, and most installations work without a C-wire with these adapters. Check your current wiring before buying.

How Much Can You Actually Save?

The EPA estimates smart thermostats save an average of 8% on heating and cooling bills annually. For a household spending $1,500/year on HVAC, that's $120/year — meaning a $200 thermostat pays for itself in under 2 years.

Nest vs Ecobee — Which is Better?

Both are excellent. Nest wins on design and ease of use. Ecobee wins on features (room sensors, HomeKit support, built-in Alexa). If you're deep in the Google ecosystem, get Nest. If you want the most features or use Apple HomeKit, get Ecobee.
