Pressure Washer Buying Guide

Electric vs Gas — Which Should You Get?

Electric pressure washers are quieter, lighter, require zero maintenance, and are perfect for most homeowners. If you're cleaning decks, cars, patio furniture, or siding a few times a year, electric is the right choice. Gas pressure washers deliver significantly more power and don't need an outlet, making them better for large properties, heavy-duty jobs, or professional use.

Understanding PSI and GPM

PSI (pounds per square inch) measures water pressure — higher PSI cuts through dirt faster. GPM (gallons per minute) measures flow rate — higher GPM rinses surfaces faster. The real cleaning power is PSI × GPM, so a 2000 PSI / 2.0 GPM washer cleans more effectively than a 3000 PSI / 1.0 GPM machine despite the lower PSI rating.

What PSI Do You Need?

Under 1500 PSI: Cars, patio furniture, light rinsing. 1500–2000 PSI: Decks, fences, siding. 2000–3000 PSI: Driveways, concrete, heavy staining. 3000+ PSI: Paint stripping, commercial use.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a pressure washer damage my deck or siding?

Yes, if you use too high a PSI or hold the nozzle too close. For wood decks, use 1200–1500 PSI max with a 25° or 40° nozzle. For vinyl siding, 1300–1600 PSI works well. Always test a small area first.

Do I need to buy separate detergent?

Most pressure washers work with standard car wash soap or deck cleaner. Buy detergent specifically labeled "pressure washer safe" — regular dish soap can damage the pump seals.
